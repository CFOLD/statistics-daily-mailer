## 해설

- This is a 2 × 3 design with one between-participants factor (coaching condition) and one repeated factor (week). There are 20 participants total, with 10 in each coaching condition.
- For the between-participants main effect, the numerator degrees of freedom are a − 1 = 2 − 1 = 1. The error term is Subjects within condition, with df = N − a = 20 − 2 = 18. Thus:
  - Coaching condition df = 1
  - Subjects within condition df = 18
- For the repeated main effect, the numerator degrees of freedom are b − 1 = 3 − 1 = 2. Its error term is Week × Subjects within condition, with df = (b − 1)(N − a) = 2 × 18 = 36. Thus:
  - Week df = 2
  - Week × Subjects within condition df = 36
- For the interaction, the numerator degrees of freedom are (a − 1)(b − 1) = 1 × 2 = 2, and the denominator is again Week × Subjects within condition with df = 36. Thus:
  - Coaching condition × Week df = 2

- The full F tests are:
  - coaching condition: F(1, 18) = 48 / 8 = 6.00, p = .025
  - week: F(2, 36) = 18 / 2 = 9.00, p = .0015
  - coaching condition × week: F(2, 36) = 8 / 2 = 4.00, p = .028

- Because the interaction is significant, the most appropriate follow-up is to examine simple effects or pairwise comparisons within each coaching condition across weeks (or compare coaching conditions separately at each week), rather than interpreting only the overall main effects. A multiple-comparison correction such as Bonferroni is needed because several follow-up comparisons inflate the familywise Type I error rate if each comparison is tested without adjustment.
